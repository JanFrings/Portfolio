Draft impact on quarterback careers

The end goal: Evaluation on the importance of the draft round for an NFL Quarterback and if it is an indicater of the players skills.

Collecting data

Data analyses start with a set of data. To work with data of my choice, I used the scraper tool Beautiful Soup and the website www.pro-football-reference.com. To make a choice of which kind of data to collect I looked at various factors:

• The period of time to investigate
• The kind and the amount of data
• Which Quarterbacks to look at and what kind of stats to collect
• The question of how to define success as a Quarterback

The time period had to represent the near past and present. But also be long enough to cover the process long time professionals are going through in the league. I choose the turn of the millennium as the starting point. To reduce the amount of data I decided to compare successful Quarterbacks with one another, rather than all Players that have been drafted since then. I came to the conclusion that there was not only one indicator of success but a variety.
Divided into two main groups:

• Quarterback related stats
• Team achievements (Postseasons & Super Bowl attempts).

This left me with the conditions to scrape data from draft classes 2000 ongoing, of players that have started one or more postseason games.
Regarding the Quarterback stats I mainly looked at the factors used to calculate the NFL passer rating:

• Pass attempts
• Pass completions
• Passing yards
• Passing touchdowns
• Passing interceptions

Additional:

• Games attended as starting Quarterback
• Draft round

Drawing insights

The collected and cleaned data had now to be explored. I used various methods to gain insights and search for visible patterns to make generalized predictions beyond the sample set.

This allows the assumption that Quarterbacks that make it into the league are going to average a predictable ratio of completions, yards, touchdowns, and even interceptions given a certain amount of attempts. Furthermore, the negative correlation of the draft round to the other stats illustrates that a smaller (earlier) draft round leads to increased Qb related stats.

The insights we gained through the correlation heatmap emphasize that the stats are highly based on the number of attempts and that this deserves differentiation in more detail. Moreover, if the first premise is true that means the negative correlation of the draft round to all the other stats is also based on the attempts or rather on the number of games a quarterback gets to start.

The conclusion we can draw is that the Quarterbacks drafted early on, not necessarily differ a lot in their ability to produce results. However, the organizations that draft Quarterbacks initially in general expected them to be able to lead an NFL team as soon as possible. This leads through more experience early on in their career which then results in more consistent results. And over time in a more complete player who can also compete in the postseason and onto the super bowl.
